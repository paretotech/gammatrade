Mechanical stop discipline · selected per trade at entry
strategy_rules.md Tier 1

Pick the stop discipline on the New Entry form. The engine ratchets the stop level per the chosen discipline (one-way; never moves down). Quantity reduces as each TP fires; when stop fires, all remaining TPs auto-cancel.

BE stop Entry, never moves. Default. After each TP fires, qty reduces; level stays at entry. If price drops back to entry, residual exits at zero loss for that portion. +$33/$1K OOS validated.
Half-MFE Trail at 50% of high once MFE ≥ entry × 1.5. Below the trigger, behaves like BE. Above, ratchets up. Never below entry. ~+$2/$1K incremental over BE — overlaps mostly.
Custom trail User picks trigger MFE % and trail %. Stop = MFE × trail %, activated once MFE ≥ entry × (1 + trigger). Same one-way ratchet, never below entry.
None No automated stop. Position can ride to -100% on residual. Use sparingly — sized-for-zero swings only.
Wired in automation/orders.py:evaluate_position_orders() and automation/rules.py:compute_stop_level(). Of 235 historical trades where the residual went bad, 234 touched entry first — which is why BE stop is the default.
Roll strike mechanics
configurable in rules.yaml · roll_strike_rules

When a roll fires (TP1/TP2/TP3 hit + roll plan triggered), the new position uses the next strike from the exited contract.

Calls
Roll UP to the next strike above.
NVDA 215C → 216C
MU 600C → 601C
SPX 7340C → 7345C
Puts
Roll DOWN to the next strike below.
AAPL 290P → 289P
NVDA 200P → 199P
SPX 7300P → 7295P
Strike interval: $5 for SPX; $1 for everything else. Offsets default to 1 (one strike) — bump call_offset_strikes or put_offset_strikes in rules.yaml to roll further OTM. Wired in automation/rules.py:roll_next_strike().
